Minimal pairs

Pairs of words which vary only by one phoneme. When two segments are in identical environment and contrast, then they must belong to different phonemes. (eg. /pin/ and /bin/)

Criteria for classifying sounds as member of the same phoneme:

  1. Phonetics similarity: Allophones of a phoneme must share phonetics properties: voice, point and manner of articulation.
  2. Complementary distribution: Where one occurs, the others should not. For example,in English, [tʰ] occurs in the initial position of the word. [t] occurs after initial [s] and [tº] occurs in final position.
  3. Free variation: Occasionally allophones might vary from person to person and occasion to occasion.It is possible that two sounds occur in the same environment without changing the meaning. E.g.: incalculable [ɪnk...] or [ɪŋk…]

Minimal sets

Group of words that can be differentiated each one from the others by changing one phoneme. (e.g. /pɪn/ vs /bɪn/ vs /tɪn/ vs /dɪn/ vs /kɪn/). A paradigm of minimal phonological contrasts is a set of words differing only by one speech sound.
