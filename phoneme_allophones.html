Phoneme

It is a mental abstract representation of a speech sounds and it is always represented between slashes. E.g.: /p/. It does not have a meaning by its own but it has a differential function because it makes a difference in meaning. Such differences are usually demonstrated by the existence of minimal pairs.

Allophone

The linguistically non-significant variants of each phoneme. In other words a phoneme may be realised by more than one speech sound and the selection of each variant is usually conditioned by the phonetic environment of the phoneme.

Allophonic variation

A phoneme is a set of allophones or individual non-contrastive speech segments. Allophones are sounds, whilst a phoneme is a set of such sounds.
For example, the phoneme /p/ is a family of sounds that has three members of allophones.

/p/
[pʰ] -aspirated-: occurs in word initial position. For example: Pin, Peter.
[p] -unaspirated-: occurs after an initial /s/. For example: Spoon.
[pº]-unreleased-: occurs in final position. For example: Loop.

/t/
[tʰ]-aspirated-: occurs in word initial position. For example: Tip, table.
[t]-unaspirated-: occurs after an initial /s/. For example: Student.
[tº]-unreleased-: occurs in final position. For example: Put, cut.
[ɾ]-flap-: flap is when /t/ or /d/ occur between a stressed and unstressed vowel or between a stressed vowel followed by /r/ and an unstressed vowel. For example: butter, party.
[?]-glottal stop-: this allophone is produced by a complete contact of the vocal cords, followed by a rapid separation of this closure. For example: button, cotton.

/k/
[kʰ]-aspirated-: occurs in word initial position (carry, coat), when a stressed vowel follows (incur, inquire) or finally after /s/ (risk, ask).
[k]-unaspirated-: occurs after an initial /s/ (skin, stick) or medially after a stressed syllable (income).
[kº]-unreleased-: occurs in final position. For example: cook, look.

/d/
[ɾ]-flap-: occurs between a stressed and an unstressed vowel. For example: ladder, sadder.
[?]-glottal stop-: occurs before syllabic /n/. For example: sudden, broaden.
[d]-voiced alveolar stop-: it occurs elsewhere. For example: diet, day, different.

/l/
The clear [l]: This allophone occurs before vowels and the palatal semivowel. For example: less, allow, million.
The dark [ł]: It occurs before consonants, after vowels or in final position. For example: silk, elf, owl, role.
Voiceless [l]: It occurs after accented (aspirated) /p.k/. for example: place, plot, class, close.
