Stress Intonation

Stress

The amount of volume that we give to a particular sound, syllable or word when we say it. Stress involves a rise in air pressure; an increase in the activity of the respiratory muscles forces more air out of the lungs during the articulation of a particular syllable.

Word stress:

Every word of more than one syllable has a syllable that is emphasized more than others.
