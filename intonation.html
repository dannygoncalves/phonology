Intonation

It refers to the use of melody and the rise and fall of the voice when speaking. Each language uses patterns of pitch variation and has its own distinctive melody and intonation patterns. The pitch patterns of intonation are similar to tunes distributed over sentences in an organized and systematic way. It will tell the intentionality of the message that the speaker wants to transmit: a statement or a question. It will also indicated if the person is confident, doubtful, shy, annoyed, or impatient.

Phrase ending with a falling pitch:

  • Declarative sentences.
    Example: She is my sister. 
  •  
  • Questions that require more than a yes/no answer.
    Example: Where is my book? 

Phrase ending with a rising pitch:

  • Questions that ask for a yes/no answer.
    Example: Will you stay?   (No, I won’t)
  •  
  • Statements that express doubt or uncertainty.
    Example: I’m not positive. 

Dale & Poms (2005).
